Some say the sun doth rule the sky -
then, eyes atwinkle, I do reply:
but what of the moon, with its shadowed allure,
 its craters, its gullies, its undulant moors?
The townsfolk, they listen, then turn back to tan,
their skin become leather just as fast as it can.
But what of those stars, steady shining in time,
their number abundant, their power sublime?
With growing impatience, the townsfolk reply,
cease defending them denizens way, way up high.
I stop for a moment, fix the brim of my hat,
then step to the plate for one more at-bat.
But what of black holes, those ravenous beasts
who make of whole galaxies decadent feasts?
The townsfolk, sun-sated, pack their things up to leave,
sun glasses, sun lotions, the towels that they weave,
and I speak to no one, surrounded by night,
hoping that one day they'll all see the light.
